Prepare your audio

Pick some parts of the score or symbols you want to create sound for.

Practice your sounds.

To record your sounds, you will need to find a quiet place. You may need to close doors, shut windows and turn things off, such as fans or wait for things like clotheswashers to finish. Some places are quietest late at night or early in the morning.

You will also need a microphone. If you own a professional microphone, you should use it. Some phones, especially iPhones, have good microphones and you could also use them. Some computers may have good internal microphones, but most do not. Some headset mics may be good - or not. If you have more than one microphone option, you may need to experiment to see which sounds best.

Practice using your mic. Make sure you point the sensitive end at your mouth. Put it close enough that you get a lot of your voice, but not so close that you peak - that is, don't let your levels get into the red or let your sound distort. If you are getting a lot of sounds of breathing or wind noise from making 'p' or 'b' sounds, try holding the mic up a bit higher - even with your nose, but still pointing at your mouth.

You want your recoring to be loud enough that the levels get near the red, but never get into it. You want to be loud enough to be heard, but not so you you distort. If you're going to do a quiet sound followed by a loud sound, you can move the microphone a little bit away as you get louder.

You should record your sound in an uncompressed format. This means you could record as a wav, aif, aiff, or au. You should avoid recording as an mp3 or ogg, if you can. If your recorder lets you set bits or sample rate, 16 bit, 44.1kHz are ok or higher is also acceptable. Do not convert your files before uploading them.

When you're ready, make a recording. You should record each symbol or gesture seperately (or, if you know how, you can edit the file into small pieces later). You may end up with a lot of short recordings. To keep track of them, you may need to write down what file goes with what symbol on the page. You may want to record the same symbol a few times and pick the version you like best.

Listen to your recordings. If you hear background noise like a train passing or hear distortion, you'll want to try again. Make sure to listen to every file before you upload it. If you find a recording has a problem, drag it to a different folder or the rubbish bin so that you don't upload it by accident.

Now that you have an audio file that sounds good, you can upload it! Make sure you know which score page it goes with and where it goes on the page.

Your sounds should be made by just you. Don't upload sounds for other people. If you have a friend who wants to make sounds also, have them sign up.

Are you ready to upload?
